# delicious.modern

## Users

**Primary**: Knowledge workers, developers, and curators who save hundreds of links per year and need them organized, searchable, and accessible across devices.

**Secondary**: Casual bookmarkers migrating from browser bookmarks or Pinterest-style visual boards. Looking for something lighter than Notion but more social than browser built-ins.

**Anti-user**: People who save fewer than 10 links/year. Those expecting AI-powered auto-categorization. Users seeking rich media embeds or full-page archival (Pocket/Instapaper territory).

## Product Purpose

Social bookmarking with editorial sensibility. A modern reimagining of del.icio.us that respects the original's Unix philosophy: do one thing (organize links) exceptionally well. Fast, keyboard-friendly, and designed for people who think in tags, not folders.

## Brand

**Tone**: Quiet confidence. Editorial restraint. Tool-first, not brand-first.

**References**:
- **Pinboard** (practical minimalism, no-nonsense)
- **Linear** (speed, polish, keyboard-driven)
- **Are.na** (thoughtful curation, visual rhythm)

**Anti-references**:
- **Pocket/Instapaper** (reading-focused, article extraction)
- **Pinterest** (visual discovery, boards metaphor)
- **Notion** (all-in-one complexity)

## Strategic Principles

1. **Speed is a feature.** Every interaction should feel instant. No loading spinners in happy paths.
2. **Tags > folders.** Multidimensional organization. No forced hierarchies.
3. **Public by default, private by choice.** Discovery through shared knowledge.
4. **Keyboard > mouse.** Every action has a keyboard shortcut.
5. **Content first, chrome second.** UI recedes when not needed.

## Register

**product** — This is a tool. Design serves function. UI should disappear once learned.
